Child Safety Standards

Last Updated: November 20, 2024

Important Notice: PetMatch is intended for users aged 18 and older. We are committed to maintaining a safe environment and have zero tolerance for child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE) content.

1. Age Requirements

PetMatch is designed for and restricted to users who are 18 years of age or older. We do not knowingly collect information from or direct any of our content to children under the age of 18.

2. Child Sexual Abuse and Exploitation (CSAE) Prevention

PetMatch has implemented comprehensive measures to prevent, detect, and respond to child sexual abuse and exploitation:

  • Zero Tolerance Policy: We have a strict zero-tolerance policy for any content related to child sexual abuse or exploitation.
  • Age Verification: Users must confirm they are 18 years or older when creating an account.
  • Content Moderation: All user-generated content, including pet photos and profiles, is subject to moderation.
  • Automated Detection: We employ automated systems to detect and flag potentially harmful content.
  • Human Review: Flagged content is reviewed by trained moderators who take immediate action when necessary.

3. Reporting Mechanisms

We provide multiple ways for users and the public to report child safety concerns:

  • In-App Reporting: Users can report suspicious content or behavior directly within the app.
  • Email Contact: Reports can be sent to jaihsaldhingra@gmail.com
  • Immediate Action: All reports are reviewed promptly, and appropriate action is taken within 24 hours.

4. Response to Reports

When child safety concerns are reported, we take the following actions:

  • Immediately investigate all reports of suspected CSAE content or behavior
  • Suspend or permanently ban accounts found to be in violation
  • Remove all violating content immediately
  • Report suspected illegal activity to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) and other appropriate authorities
  • Preserve evidence for law enforcement as required by law
  • Cooperate fully with law enforcement investigations

5. Compliance with Laws and Regulations

PetMatch complies with all relevant child safety laws and regulations, including:

  • Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA)
  • Reporting requirements to national and regional authorities
  • Cooperation with law enforcement agencies worldwide
  • International standards for child protection online

6. Reporting to Authorities

We maintain partnerships and reporting relationships with:

  • National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC): We report suspected CSAM to NCMEC's CyberTipline as required by law.
  • Local Law Enforcement: We work with local, state, and federal law enforcement agencies to combat child exploitation.
  • International Authorities: We cooperate with international law enforcement agencies and child protection organizations.

7. Staff Training

Our team receives regular training on:

  • Identifying child sexual abuse material (CSAM)
  • Recognizing grooming behaviors and exploitation patterns
  • Proper reporting procedures and legal requirements
  • Trauma-informed response protocols

8. Privacy and Data Protection

While protecting children is our priority, we also maintain user privacy:

  • We only collect necessary information for safety purposes
  • User data is protected in accordance with our Privacy Policy
  • We balance safety with privacy rights in all our practices

9. Platform Purpose

PetMatch is exclusively designed for connecting people with stray and abandoned pets for adoption purposes. Our platform:

  • Focuses solely on pet adoption and rescue
  • Is not a social networking or dating platform for minors
  • Restricts content to pet-related posts and adoption information
  • Monitors all user interactions for safety and appropriateness

10. Continuous Improvement

We are committed to continuously improving our child safety practices:

  • Regular audits of our safety systems
  • Updates to detection technologies
  • Engagement with child safety organizations
  • Adaptation to emerging threats and best practices

These child safety standards may be updated periodically to reflect new practices, technologies, or legal requirements. The "Last Updated" date at the top of this page indicates when the most recent changes were made.
